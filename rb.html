ACHARI PANEER TIKKA ROLL

Achari Paneer Tikka Roll is a delicious and flavorful Indian dish that combines the goodness of paneer (Indian cottage cheese) with achari (pickle) flavors, all rolled up in a roti or flatbread. Here's a simple recipe for you to try:

Ingredients:

For Achari Paneer Tikka:

  • 250g paneer, cut into cubes
  • 2 tablespoons thick yogurt
  • 1 tablespoon mustard oil
  • 1 tablespoon pickling spice mix (achari masala)
  • 1 teaspoon red chili powder
  • 1 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • 1 teaspoon garam masala
  • 1 teaspoon cumin powder
  • Salt to taste
  • 1 tablespoon lemon juice
  • 1 tablespoon gram flour (besan)

For the Roll:

  • Rotis or flatbreads
  • Sliced onions, tomatoes, and cucumbers for garnish
  • Mint chutney or your favorite sauce

Instructions:

For Achari Paneer Tikka:

In a bowl, mix yogurt, mustard oil, pickling spice mix, red chili powder, turmeric powder, garam masala, cumin powder, salt, lemon juice, and gram flour to make the marinade. Add the paneer cubes to the marinade, ensuring each piece is well-coated. Let it marinate for at least 30 minutes or longer in the refrigerator. Preheat your oven to 200°C (392°F). Thread the marinated paneer cubes onto skewers and place them on a baking tray lined with parchment paper. Bake in the preheated oven for 15-20 minutes or until the paneer is cooked and has a slight char on the edges. You can also grill them on a barbecue for that smoky flavor.
